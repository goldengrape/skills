## Y Combinator (YC) Evaluation Standards

### The Four Pillars of Evaluation
YC focuses on four core elements, with the highest weight placed on verifiable numbers:

| Pillar | Weight | Focus | Benchmark |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. The Idea** | 10% | Clarity, Innovation, Logicality | Can explain in one sentence |
| **2. The Market** | 20% | TAM, Potential for Growth | TAM > $1.0 Billion |
| **3. The Team** | 30% | Execution, Resilience, Fit | "Cockroach" tenacity |
| **4. Traction** | 40% | Growth rate, Retention | 5-7% Weekly Growth |

**Core Insight**: Traction is the most heavily weighted (40%) because it is the only objective evidence.

## The Sequoia Capital Investment Memo Framework

### 1. Company Purpose
Refine the mission into a short, punchy sentence.
- ‚úÖ **Airbnb**: "Belong anywhere."
- ‚úÖ **Stripe**: "Increase the GDP of the internet."

### 2. Problem Statement
- **Pain Score**: 1-3 (Annoyance), 4-6 (Significant pain), 7-9 (Acute misery), 10 (Hair on fire).
- **Sequoia Benchmark**: Must be a score of 7+. If the problem isn't "on fire," people won't pay.

### 3. Solution
**The 10x Rule**: A product must be 10x better than the incumbent (in price, speed, or quality) to overcome the friction of switching.

### 4. Competitive Advantage (The Moat)
Identify the "Unfair Advantage":
- **Network Effects**: Value increases with every user (Facebook).
- **Data Moat**: Unique datasets that improve the product (Google Search).
- **Switching Costs**: Integration is so deep it's painful to leave (Salesforce).

### 5. Business Model (Unit Economics)
- **LTV / CAC Calculation**: LTV (Lifetime Value) divided by CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost).
- **Healthy Threshold**: **LTV/CAC > 3**.
- **Payback Period**: How many months to recover CAC? (Healthy < 12 months).

## "Devil's Advocate" Mode (Critical Audit)

When switched to this mode, the Navigator will attack the business's weakest links:
1. **The Scale Trap**: "Will your CAC rise as you move beyond early adopters?"
2. **The Incumbent Attack**: "If Google/Amazon clones this tomorrow, how do you survive?"
3. **The 'Mom' Test**: "Who, besides your colleagues and family, has actually paid for this? Show me the Stripe invoice."

## Key Principles Summary

1. **Traction Over Everything**: Growth data is the only truth.
2. **The 10x Improvement Rule**: Don't build "marginal" improvements.
3. **LTV/CAC > 3**: A non-negotiable standard for business sustainability.
4. **Cockroach Tenacity**: Founders must be the most resilient part of the system.
5. **5-7% Weekly**: This is the heart rate of a healthy startup.
